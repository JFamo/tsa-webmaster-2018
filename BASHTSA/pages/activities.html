Activities

Boyertown Senior High School TSA is constantly planning fun and exciting activities
for its members, both to raise money and have a good time.

Homecoming Carnival

Each year during the school’s Homecoming Carnival, the Boyertown Area Senior High School TSA runs a stand selling food, fun, and opportunities to win prizes. For the price of a few tickets, carnival-goers can eat hot dogs or walking tacos, or even enjoy a cold water or soda. The stand consisted of TSA members volunteering time after school and into the evening to make food, handle tickets, and enjoy the festivities.

In the week leading up to the Homecoming game, the entire school participated in Spirit Week, and the BASH TSA Chapter was not afraid to sport their school pride! Members participated in America Monday, Tacky Tourist Day, [Holiday Day?], [thursday?], and [Class day?], and worked tirelessly to develop fun games and prizes for the yearly carnival.

TSA’s representation at the carnival cemented its image to students, staff, and the community that it is more than just a technology club. While winning awards at the regional, state, and national levels can turn local heads, simply showing the community compassion by offering a cold drink also makes a powerful statement. Wherever there is a chance to lift spirits and stir up some fun in the local area, BASH TSA is always there!

Spaghetti Dinner

In early December, BASH TSA hosts a yearly spaghetti buffet to friends and families of TSA members, as well as teachers, people of the community, and even school board members. The all-you-can-eat dinner includes mouth-watering spaghetti, a side salad, fluffy buttered rolls, a variety of drinks, and a delicious assortment of homemade desserts. At $10 a meal, including to-go boxes, it is always a bargain and a popular fundraiser for the community and TSA members.

TSA students are encouraged to sell as many tickets as they can in the weeks leading up to the event, though tickets can also be bought at the door. Profits from pre-sold tickets go directly to individuals to finance their trip to the state conference in April. By offering this fundraising opportunity, BASH TSA allows students to more effectively find the necessary funds. With a love of technology comes a love of giving the best opportunities to student who pursue it, and because of this, the BASH TSA Spaghetti Dinner is always a huge success.

Bag Bingo

At the suggestion of the 2016-17 fundraising committee, BASH TSA developed a bag bingo night for addition fundraising for the state conference. By selling tickets ahead of time and online, the event was sold out within a few weeks. On the day of, students joined together to make and serve food through donations, help run the event itself, and clean up afterwards. It included a 50/50 ticket raffle, and many vendors brought their wares to the bingo night, creating a busy, successful atmosphere that lasted the entire evening. The profits of $4,200.00 went to each student who volunteered their time to run the event to fund the students’ PA TSA State conference trip. As well as this, the chapter was able to work alongside the NORCO Fire Department, it’s first involvement with a local community organization. The chapter looks forward to running the event in years to come.

BASH TSA strives to enable students to pursue their love of technology and refuses to let any financial difficulties get in the way of that passion. A commitment to the finding and founding of fundraising opportunities defines and elevates success at BASH TSA.

Painting with a Twist

To drum up some hype within the club, a Painting With A Twist party was organized near the beginning of the year. Members enjoyed an evening of creative expression, snacks, and fellowship, including participating in social media and painting competitions. Getting together to paint reinforced friendship as not only a chapter, but as a team. Students became closer with old pals and made new connections while laughing and socializing outside of the technology education rooms. An important part of success as a TSA chapter is the unity between members, and BASH TSA always makes sure of this.

Regional Conference 2016-2017

Boyertown schools enjoyed a strong representation at the 2016-17 Regional Conference, securing a large variety of awards, including the Inspiration Award. Chapter members competed in every single event, and spent a busy day viewing fellow members compete. BASH-TSA members were represented at every special interest session, from the 'Running for State Office' session to the Chapter Officer meet-and-greet. Senior chapter members setup a router to host a 'meme server', which any other attendees could connect to and share memes with. At the end of the day BASH-TSA returned with ideas, motivation, and excitement for the state conference.

PA-TSA State Conference 2016-2017

At the 2017 PA TSA State conference, BASH-TSA focused on not only achieving success, but celebrating hard work. After all events were turned in, interviews were wrapped up, and naps were taken, the entire chapter gathered in the courtyard to participate in a mock wedding. With the happy couple ready to go, everyone lined up alongside the sidewalk as our advisor walked the “bride” down the aisle. Onlookers from other chapters watched from the balconies, and it became a memorable experience for everyone.

Later in the conference, the majority of BASH-TSA gathered in the robotics building to cheer on the Boyertown team's robot, affectionately named 'Sergio'. Chanting and cheering filled the hall, inspiring the Boyertown team and demonstrating the exuberance of our chapter.

Throughout the conference, BASH-TSA students worked on having fun and earning trophies. Chapter members relished in the even spread of success, fun, and bonding.

National Conference 2016-2017

At the 2017 TSA National Conference in Orlando, Florida, BASH-TSA enjoyed success and fun in the sun. The chapter often stopped by the pool and enjoyed many games of volleyball with other chapters, though no one was particularly good at it. Members of the chapter attended a LEAP Seminar to further their leadership knowledge. The chapter gathered at 4:30 in the morning the day before Nationals to depart, driving for ten hours to Savannah Georgia, where they went out to eat and walked along the historic river walk. The next day the chapter continued to Orlando, and spent a successful conference competing, relaxing, and soaking in the Florida sun. On the return trip, the chapter again stopped in Savannah, where members participated in karaoke, went to the beach, and had a late-night party watching The Twilight Zone.

In the end, BASH-TSA members still reminisce on the time spent with each other at the National Conference, as the chapter enjoyed unforgettable memories.
